⚔️ Combat System
THE RPS TRIANGLE

Every round, both you and your opponent choose one of three moves simultaneously. The outcome determines who scores a round point.

MoveBeatsLoses To
⚔️ Strike✨ Spell🛡️ Shield
🛡️ Shield⚔️ Strike✨ Spell
✨ Spell🛡️ Shield⚔️ Strike
BEST-OF-3 ROUNDS

Each battle is decided in best-of-3 round scoring. No HP damage is dealt until someone wins 2 rounds.

OutcomeDamage Dealt
2-0 Sweep100% ATK burst damage
2-1 Win75% ATK burst damage
Tie / Clash15% ATK chip to both — no round point

After a burst lands, the round score resets and the fight continues until a fighter reaches 0 HP.

HOW DAMAGE WORKS

A 2-0 sweep hits harder than a 2-1 win — winning decisively always matters. Ties deal chip damage to both fighters, and your SPD stat reduces how much chip you absorb on a clash.

DEF reduces incoming burst damage but is capped — even a heavily armoured fighter feels every hit. MAG adds bonus damage to your Spell wins. LCK is your crit chance — higher LCK means more frequent hard-hitting strikes.

SPD — CLASH REDUCTION

When a round ties (clash), SPD advantage gives a chance to take reduced chip damage.

SPD AdvantageChance of Reduced ChipReduction
1–525%50% of normal chip
6–1240%50% of normal chip
13+55% (hard cap)50% of normal chip
SPD never guarantees zero chip damage. At 55% it's still a coin flip — just a heavily weighted one.
ENEMY INTELLIGENCE

Enemies don't fight the same way forever. As your legend grows, opponents think harder — noticing patterns, adapting, punishing predictability. What works in the Meadow Pit won't keep working in Champions League Arena.

If you find yourself losing to the same move repeatedly, mix it up. The enemy may be reading you.

LEVEL-UP GAINS

Every level gained increases your ATK, DEF, and max HP. The gains are modest per level — gear is the primary driver of power, not raw levelling.

ENEMY SCALING

Enemies scale with your level and your gear score. The better your equipment (rarity and upgrade level), the harder enemies hit and the more HP they have. Farming lower arenas with top gear gets progressively harder — they're designed to push you forward, not stay easy forever.

Boss HP scales faster than their other stats. High-rarity gear gives enemies a meaningful ATK and HP boost to match your power.

UPGRADING

Gear can be upgraded from +1 to +10. Each tier boosts the item's stats. Cost increases with each tier — early upgrades are cheap, pushing gear to +10 is a serious gold investment. Upgrading higher-rarity gear is generally better value than upgrading commons.

Upgrades can be done directly from the Equip tab — no need to visit Inventory first. You can also enchant equipped gear from the same screen.

ALPHA SPAWNS

When entering any arena there is a 30% chance an Alpha enemy replaces one of the normal enemies. Alphas have boosted stats, always drop something on defeat, and have a strong (40%) chance of dropping a Relic.

If an Alpha is lurking, it persists until you fight it — exiting and re-entering the arena won't reset it. The arena card will warn you it's there.

GEMS 💎

Gems are the premium currency. They're earned through Achievements and the Battle Pass free track — no purchase required, though you can buy them directly if you want to move faster.

Use them for Healing Potions when you've hit your daily gold limit, to protect a streak you've built up, or to unlock the Premium Battle Pass. They're scarce by design — spend them on what matters to you.

SHOP COMPOSITION

The shop always stocks exactly: 1 Common · 2 Uncommon · 1 Rare — plus up to 3 Healing Potions. All three gear slots (Weapon, Armor, Ring) are always represented. Stock refreshes every 24 hours. Prices have ±15% variance each cycle.

📈 Progression
XP & LEVELLING

You earn XP from every fight. Higher arenas give more XP per battle, and bosses give the most. The XP required per level increases as you grow — early levels are fast, later levels take longer.

Enemy TypeXP Reward
Meadow Pit normal25–35 XP
Manor Yard normal50–60 XP
Small Amphitheater normal70–80 XP
Palace Arena normal90–105 XP
Champions League Arena normal120–135 XP
Any Boss95–250 XP (scales with arena)
BATTLE PASS XP

Every battle earns Battle Pass XP. Alphas and bosses are worth more. After reaching Tier 30 you enter the Proving Grounds — each additional tier earns 1 Gem.

HEALTH REGEN

Your HP regenerates at 1 point per minute — and it keeps ticking even when the game is closed. Drop to 1 HP after a brutal fight, close the game, come back 99 minutes later and you're at full. Patience is a valid strategy.

HP does not restore between enemies mid-run. What you leave one fight with is what you carry into the next. Regen only kicks in when you're back at the Ludus.

⚔️ Tournament
HOW IT WORKS

The Tournament is The Eternal Three's ranked mode. Choose an opponent from the matchmaking pool, fight a standard battle, and earn or lose RP (Rating Points) based on the result. Your RP determines your tier.

Unlike arenas, Tournament battles have no entry fee and don't consume arena run resources. Your HP still carries through — don't enter a Tournament match on low HP.

PROMOTION & DEMOTION

Cross a tier threshold and you're promoted or demoted immediately. There is no floor protection — you can drop all the way from God to Bronze if you lose enough.

The one exception: demotion protection. The first time you drop to a tier floor after promoting into a new tier, you're protected once. One free loss. After that, demotion is live.

The arena doesn't care about your feelings. Neither does the ranking system. Win or fall.
LEADERBOARD

The Tournament tab shows your global rank among all players and your position within your current tier. God tier holds fewer than 50 players at any time — reaching it is a genuine achievement.

📡 Live leaderboard with real player data unlocks at launch. Rankings in the prototype are simulated based on your RP.
STRATEGY

Opponent difficulty is shown before you commit. Easy opponents give less RP but are safer. Hard opponents give more RP but hurt more when you lose. Choose based on your HP and confidence.

Your class and gear matter here just as much as in the arenas. The Tournament uses the same combat system — the opponent just doesn't follow arena enemy patterns.
